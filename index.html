Adoption gives animals a second chance to have safe and loving homes. Adopting pets is a way to support local shelters instead of supporting breeding facilities or puppy mills that have cruel practices.Adopting a pet from a shelter saves more than just one life. By adopting, you are helping to free up space in the shelter, which gives the shelter more space to rescue more animals. Adopting an animal means you are getting a pet that has received appropriate medical care. Most shelters will vaccinate, spay/neuter, and microchip animals before they are adopted. Adopting animals supports community animal shelters. Animal shelters often provide services beyond pet adoption. For example, Crossroads Animal Shelter offers numerous veterinary services to the community to help ensure animal well-being.
